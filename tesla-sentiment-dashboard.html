What is "sentiment" and how was it measured?

A computer program called an NLP (Natural Language Processing) model read every post and comment and gave it a score between −1.0 and +1.0. Think of it like a mood meter — the more positive the language used, the higher the score. A score near 0 means the text was balanced or factual. Scores above +0.05 are positive, below −0.05 are negative.

Does Writing More = Being More Positive?
📏 Longer Comments Tend to Be More Positive
We grouped comments by how long they were (in characters) and looked at the average mood score for each length group. Longer, more detailed replies are consistently more constructive.
💡Very long comments (600+ characters) score 4× higher than very short ones. A quick "this sucks" reaction is very different from a 600-word thoughtful critique.
📝 vs 💬 — Posts Are More Positive Than Comments
Original posts average a score of +0.43 while comments average just +0.23. This gap shows how discussion threads can amplify negativity even when the original post was positive.
💡This is a common pattern in online communities — the original poster is more thoughtful, while comments react more emotionally, pulling the average down.
