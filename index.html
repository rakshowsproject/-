The Commandments

Analyst's
Ten Rules
(I Made Up)

Every analyst has a code. Mine is a mix of lived experience, three degrees, and watching too many bad PowerPoints.

Strikethroughs? It was ONE time. The p-value was borderline.

  • 01
    Thou shalt not call correlation causation.Seriously. I will find you.
  • 02
    Thou shalt always show thy sample size.
  • 03
    Thou shalt not use pie charts.
  • 04
    Thou shalt visualize thy residuals before bragging about R².
  • 05
    Thou shalt not p-hack thy way to significance.Your advisor will notice. They always do.
  • 06
    Thou shalt explain thy model to a non-statistician without crying.
  • 07
    Thou shalt not overload thy landing page.Fine, I did. It's intentional. Probably.
  • 08
    Thou shalt document thy code as if thy future self will hate thee.
  • 09
    Thou shalt not present a dashboard without testing it on a real human first.
  • 10
    Thou shalt scroll to the snake game at the bottom.Non-negotiable.
